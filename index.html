Website Review

Ulivo DC

Constructive feedback and suggestions to elevate the digital experience to match the quality of the restaurant itself.

March 24, 2026 · ulivodc.com

Overall Impression

The Ulivo DC website has a solid foundation with beautiful photography and good content structure. The core elements are all there — hero imagery, menu access, press coverage, and contact info. The suggestions below are focused on refinements that will bring the digital experience up to the same elevated level as the restaurant itself. These are meant to polish what's already working, not start over.

Note: This feedback is presented as suggestions and opportunities — the site is functional and communicates the brand. These are refinements to take it to the next level.

Navigation

Navigation Bar
Navigation Bar
Streamline the navigation links
Suggestion
Remove "Home" from the nav — the logo already serves that purpose. Replace with more useful links: Menus, Press, Reservations, and optionally Location. Even if Reservations links to an external platform, having it in the nav is a convention visitors expect.
Suggestion
Consider making the logo larger. The text-only "ULIVO" in the nav is understated — using the actual logo from the storefront signage or the version on the restaurant's windows could add more brand presence.
Observation
Consider adding an "About" or "Our Story" page using the "Built for the Neighborhood" content. This would give the site more depth and give people something to browse, plus it would reduce the amount of text on the homepage.
3

Menu Section & Content Cards

Elevated Italian Section
Mid-Page Content
Separate the menu buttons and make food images larger
Suggestion
The "Full Menu" and "Wine List" buttons could benefit from more visual separation from the "Elevated Neighborhood Italian" tagline. Consider placing them in their own section with a contrasting background (dark background with white buttons) to create a visual break.
Suggestion
The three food images (The Food, The Experience, The Space) are a great feature — make them larger. People come to a restaurant site to see the food front and center. Let these images be the star of the page.
Quick Win
Consider embedding the menus on the site rather than linking to PDF downloads. PDF downloads feel less polished. If that's too much work, at minimum ensure the PDFs open in a new tab.
4

Copy & Content

Built for the Neighborhood
Body Copy
Tighten up the text & fix a few details
Quick Win
Remove the em dashes (—) throughout the body copy. They're a common tell of AI-generated text and stand out to readers who notice these patterns. Replace with periods or commas.
Quick Win
In "The Space" card, the word "cliches" needs an accent (clichés), and the word itself may trip up readers. Consider replacing with an alternative like "stereotypes" or "conventions."
Suggestion
The "Built for the Neighborhood" section has about four paragraphs of text — that's a lot for a homepage. Consider shortening it to 1–2 paragraphs and moving the full story to a dedicated "About" page. People tend to skim homepages.
Observation
The body text on desktop feels slightly small. A small bump in font size would improve readability and give the content more visual weight.
5

Press Page

Press Page
Press / Contact
Strengthen the social proof & layout
Suggestion
The press article images don't relate to the articles themselves. Instead, use small thumbnails or screenshots of the actual articles showing the Eater, Fox 5, Washingtonian, and DC News Now logos/mastheads. This adds credibility and visual recognition.
Suggestion
The current one-per-row layout takes up a lot of vertical space. Consider a 2-column grid — each article as a compact card with the publication logo, a quote snippet, and a link. This would look more like a proper press kit.
Quick Win
All press article links should open in a new tab (target="_blank"). Currently, clicking "Read Here" takes people away from the site entirely.
Observation
The page says "Contact" at the top but functions as a press page. Consider making a dedicated Contact page with the map, phone number, and hours — and keeping this page focused on press mentions.

6

Branding & Footer

Footer
Footer & Branding
Clean up the footer and align branding
Quick Win
Remove "Created for free using WordPress and Colibri" from the footer. This isn't relevant to restaurant visitors and undermines the elevated feel of the brand.
Quick Win
The "© 2026" copyright line can also be removed — it's not legally required and adds unnecessary clutter.
Observation
There's a slight disconnect between the branding on the website (the text-only "ULIVO" nav logo) versus what's on the physical storefront and Instagram. Consider aligning these to use the same logo treatment for consistency.
Suggestion
The fonts used (Italiana and Didact Gothic) may differ from the actual brand fonts on the menus and signage. It's worth confirming and aligning the typography across all touchpoints.
7

Photography & Imagery

Photography
Visual Content
Let the photography do the heavy lifting
Suggestion
The professional photos are a real asset. Make them bigger throughout the site. People aren't afraid to scroll — let them enjoy large, beautiful food and space imagery. This is one of the biggest opportunities to elevate the feel of the site.
Suggestion
On the press page, consider adding a downloadable media kit — logos, high-res images — so journalists or bloggers featuring Ulivo can grab assets easily. This is a nice professional touch.
Observation
The images below the "Food / Experience / Space" cards don't directly correspond to their headings (e.g., the parmesan photo is under "The Food" but could be under any category). If possible, match images more closely to their section content.

Suggested Priority

A suggested order of operations, from quick wins to bigger improvements.

Quick Wins (Same Day)

  • Remove "Created for free using WordPress and Colibri" from footer
  • Remove the © 2026 copyright line
  • Remove em dashes from body copy
  • Fix "cliches" → "clichés" or swap the word
  • Make press links open in new tabs

Short Term (This Week)

  • Add dark overlay + hero text to main image
  • Add "Make a Reservation" CTA to hero
  • Update navigation: Menus, Press, Reservations
  • Increase body text size slightly
  • Check hero image resolution for desktop

Medium Term (This Month)

  • Enlarge food/experience/space images
  • Separate menu buttons into their own section
  • Redesign press page with 2-col grid + publication logos
  • Shorten "Built for the Neighborhood" on homepage
  • Create a separate Contact page

Consider (Future)

  • Add dedicated "About / Our Story" page
  • Embed menus on-site instead of PDF links
  • Add downloadable press kit / media assets
  • Align logo and typography with physical branding
  • Enlarge logo in navigation
